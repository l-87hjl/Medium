The 12 Anomalies of 3I/ATLAS

(Organized Around Avi Loeb’s Framework, with Observations and Interpretations)

The interstellar object 3I/ATLAS has drawn attention not just because it is only the third known visitor from outside the solar system, but because of a cluster of unusual features. Harvard astrophysicist Avi Loeb has organized these into a list of twelve “anomalies,” which he argues are collectively difficult to explain with familiar comet physics alone.

The primary technical framing for these anomalies comes from his Medium essay “Do the Anomalies of 3I/ATLAS Flag Alien Technology or an Unfamiliar Interstellar Iceberg?” , together with follow-up posts such as “The 12th Anomaly of 3I/ATLAS: Orientation of the Jets Is Not Smeared by Rotation” .

What follows is a reader oriented overview of those twelve anomalies. For each one:

  • Loeb’s own framing is summarized.
  • The way news and popular coverage tend to describe the same feature is highlighted.
  • Key observations and instruments are listed.
  • Mainstream and alternative interpretations are briefly outlined.

A final section gathers additional oddities that do not fit cleanly into a single category.

1. Retrograde Ecliptic Alignment

Loeb’s explanation

Loeb emphasizes that 3I/ATLAS travels on a retrograde path, moving opposite the direction of the planets, yet remains aligned to within about 5 degrees of the plane in which the planets orbit (the ecliptic). He estimates the probability of such a configuration at roughly 0.2 percent for a random interstellar object.

How the media usually describe it

  • A “backwards orbit” or a comet “coming in the wrong way.”
  • A path so hyperbolic and fast that, when plotted, it looks almost like a straight line slicing through the solar system.

Observations and instruments

  • Orbital calculations give an inclination of roughly 175 degrees, meaning a strongly retrograde orbit that still lies close to the solar system’s flat disk. Basic orbital parameters are summarized on the 3I/ATLAS Wikipedia page.
  • The eccentricity is highly hyperbolic, indicating that 3I/ATLAS is unbound and merely passing through.
  • These parameters are derived from astrometric data taken by discovery surveys such as ATLAS and follow-up observatories worldwide.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb treats the combination, retrograde plus closely aligned with the ecliptic, as statistically noteworthy, arguing that it may be an indicator of something other than a random natural visitor.
  • Many dynamicists counter that observational bias favors discovering interstellar objects that cross the inner solar system near the ecliptic, so alignment alone is not considered especially suspicious.
  • This anomaly is about geometry rather than the physical make-up of the object.

2. Persistent Sunward Jet (Anti-tail)

Loeb’s explanation

Loeb notes that during July, August, and again in early November 2025, 3I/ATLAS displayed a prominent sunward jet, an “anti-tail,” which he argues cannot be explained away as a mere line-of-sight illusion, unlike most classical anti-tails in comets. He expands on this in “The Remarkable Large-Scale Structure of Anti-Tail and Tail Jets from 3I/ATLAS” .

How the media usually describe it

  • “Tail pointing toward the Sun” or “forward-pointing tail.”
  • A bright “spike” extending toward the Sun while a more conventional tail stretches away.

Observations and instruments

  • Deep images from early November show narrow jets extending roughly a million kilometers sunward and several million kilometers anti-sunward, based on long-exposure amateur and pro-am imagery summarized in Loeb’s Medium posts.
  • Solar-observing spacecraft (such as SOHO and STEREO) and wide-field imagers also record asymmetric structures near perihelion, with both sunward and anti-solar components.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb regards the persistent, geometrically consistent sunward feature as a genuine jet configuration that is difficult to reconcile with ordinary dust dynamics and perspective effects.
  • Comet specialists generally note that anti-tails can result from a mix of dust grain sizes and viewing angle, though detailed modeling of this specific case is still evolving.
  • The debate centers on whether this particular anti-tail is qualitatively different from those seen in well-studied comets.

3. Anomalous Mass Relative to Previous Interstellar Objects

Loeb’s explanation

According to Loeb, the nucleus of 3I/ATLAS appears to be roughly a million times more massive than 1I/ʻOumuamua and about a thousand times more massive than 2I/Borisov, while also moving faster than either. He argues that this combination has a low prior probability if all three interstellar objects belong to the same natural population.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Manhattan-sized” upper-limit comparisons.
  • “Much larger than ʻOumuamua and Borisov” or “by far the biggest interstellar visitor yet seen.”

Observations and instruments

  • The NASA 3I/ATLAS fact sheet, based on Hubble data, estimates that the diameter of the nucleus is between about 440 meters and 5.6 kilometers, depending on assumptions about reflectivity (NASA 3I/ATLAS FAQ).
  • By contrast, ʻOumuamua is estimated at a few hundred meters in length, and Borisov at under a kilometer, placing 3I/ATLAS well above both in potential mass.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb focuses on the mismatch in mass and speed relative to the first two interstellar objects, treating this as another outlier in a growing cluster.
  • Other astronomers suggest that with only three known interstellar objects, it is premature to assume a “typical” size or velocity distribution; 3I/ATLAS may simply reveal that the population is broad and diverse.

4. Fine-Tuned Planetary Encounter Geometry

Loeb’s explanation

Loeb highlights that 3I/ATLAS passed within tens of millions of kilometers of Mars, will pass relatively near Venus and Jupiter in a geometric sense, and was almost exactly behind the Sun from Earth’s point of view during its perihelion, making it effectively invisible from the ground at that critical moment. He refers to this pattern as “fine-tuned” encounter geometry, with low probability in advance.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Perfect timing to fly by Mars.”
  • “Slipped behind the Sun, out of Earth’s view, right when it was closest to the Sun.”
  • “No danger to Earth, but a near miss in astronomical terms.”

Observations and instruments

  • Mars-orbiting spacecraft such as Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (HiRISE), MAVEN, and TGO observed 3I/ATLAS when it came within roughly 30 million kilometers of Mars, discussed in work cited by Loeb and in NASA mission updates.
  • Around perihelion near October 29, 2025, the object’s apparent position placed it close to the Sun on the sky from Earth, so it was primarily monitored by solar observatories such as SOHO and STEREO.
  • The closest approach to Earth in mid-December 2025 remains hundreds of millions of kilometers away, near in astrophysical terms but entirely harmless.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb treats this sequence as another statistical oddity that, taken together with the others, may warrant non-standard hypotheses.
  • The mainstream view treats the geometry as interesting but unsurprising: an interstellar orbit perturbed by the Sun’s gravity can easily result in passes reasonably close to one or more planets without implying any intent or design.

5. Unusual Nickel-to-Iron and Nickel-to-Cyanide Ratios

Loeb’s explanation

Spectroscopic studies show strong nickel emission lines in the gas surrounding 3I/ATLAS, but iron lines are weak or absent. Loeb notes that the nickel-to-iron ratio, and the nickel-to-cyanide ratio, appear to be far higher than in known comets, including 2I/Borisov.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Nickel-rich, iron-poor gas plume.”
  • “Abundance of nickel that defies expectations from Solar System comets.”

Observations and instruments

  • High-resolution spectroscopy with large telescopes such as ESO’s Very Large Telescope detects nickel emission from the coma while failing to detect corresponding iron lines, as highlighted in press pieces on 3I/ATLAS’s “unheard-of chemistry” (for example Chron.com coverage).
  • Other species such as cyanide (CN) are present, so nickel-to-CN ratios can be estimated and compared to Solar System comets.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb draws attention to the nickel-rich, iron-poor signature as an example of a composition that is atypical for natural comets, and even compares aspects of it to certain industrial nickel alloys.
  • Observing teams emphasize that unusual nickel chemistry can arise from specific compounds and temperature regimes. They interpret the result as evidence for a different formation or processing history rather than necessarily a sign of technology.

6. Extremely Low Water Content

Loeb’s explanation

Loeb notes that water appears to make up only around four percent of the gas by mass, whereas in familiar comets water is typically the dominant volatile.

How the media usually describe it

  • “CO₂-rich, water-poor interstellar comet.”
  • “One of the highest CO₂-to-water ratios ever measured.”

Observations and instruments

  • NASA’s JWST nugget on 3I/ATLAS notes an “unusually CO₂-rich coma” based on NIRSpec observations from August 6, 2025 (JWST 3I/ATLAS nugget PDF).
  • A JWST-based paper reports one of the highest CO₂/H₂O ratios ever recorded in a comet, and suggests formation near a CO₂ ice line or in a radiation-rich environment (JWST ISO CO₂/H₂O analysis).
  • Radio observations with the MeerKAT array detect hydroxyl (OH) at 18 cm, confirming that water is present but not abundant compared to other volatiles, as described in LiveScience coverage .

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb counts this as another strong departure from the profile of “normal” comets, reinforcing his anomaly list.
  • JWST researchers suggest that long-term exposure to cosmic rays in interstellar space may have chemically altered the outer layers of icy material, building a CO₂-rich “crust” over billions of years.
  • In that view, 3I/ATLAS is “water-poor on the surface” because of its deep interstellar history, not because it lacks water altogether.

7. Extreme Negative Polarization

Loeb’s explanation

Polarimetric measurements show an unusually deep “negative polarization” when sunlight reflects off dust and ice in the coma at small phase angles. Loeb notes that the depth and shape of this negative polarization branch differ from all previously studied comets, including 2I/Borisov.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Extreme negative polarization” or “an unprecedented light signature among comets.”
  • “Light scattered in a way unlike any comet seen before.”

Observations and instruments

  • Polarimetry with instruments such as VLT/FORS2 and ALFOSC reveals a minimum polarization of about minus 2.7 percent at a phase angle of roughly 7 degrees, with a rapid transition to positive polarization at higher phase angles, according to recent arXiv analyses of 3I/ATLAS polarimetry.
  • This pattern sits outside the known “families” of comets and asteroids that have been measured in polarized light.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb lists this as another “unprecedented” behavior, suggesting that the dust or ice structure near 3I/ATLAS is unusual in a way that fits his broader anomaly narrative.
  • Polarimetry teams interpret the result as evidence that 3I/ATLAS may represent a distinct type of comet, with different grain sizes, compositions, or surface textures, but still within the realm of natural variation.

8. Directional Coincidence with the “Wow!” Signal

Loeb’s explanation

The direction from which 3I/ATLAS entered the solar system lies within about nine degrees of the sky position associated with the famous 1977 “Wow!” radio signal. Loeb estimates the probability of this directional coincidence at around 0.6 percent.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Came from the same part of the sky as the Wow! signal.”
  • “Interstellar visitor from the Wow! region.”

Observations and instruments

  • Orbital calculations provide the inbound asymptotic direction of 3I/ATLAS in celestial coordinates.
  • The Wow! signal’s direction is also known to within a patch of the constellation Sagittarius. The two directions overlap within several degrees.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb includes this as a suggestive detail, noting that on its own the coincidence is weak but becomes more interesting in combination with other anomalies.
  • Radio astronomers generally regard this as an expected kind of coincidence. The sky is large but not that large when many objects and directions are considered over time, and no new radio emission has been linked to 3I/ATLAS itself.

9. Unprecedented Brightening Rate and Blue Color

Loeb’s explanation

Loeb argues that 3I/ATLAS brightened faster near perihelion than any comet previously recorded and that its color is distinctly bluer than the Sun, which he treats as a sign of unusual emission.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Brightened much faster than expected.”
  • “Changed from red to green to blue,” or “now appears as a bright, bluish fuzzball.”

Observations and instruments

  • Photometric monitoring by survey telescopes shows 3I/ATLAS outpacing brightness predictions as it neared the Sun, with a larger-than-expected rate of brightening.
  • Early observations describe a redder or dustier appearance. Later ones highlight greenish emission, often associated with diatomic carbon, and near perihelion a blue or blue-white glow dominated by gas emission. JWST and Hubble images contribute to this color story, as summarized in LiveScience JWST coverage .
  • Space-based solar observatories captured rapid brightening around perihelion, while ground-based observers tracked a rise from about magnitude 17 to around magnitude 9 or 10, never quite reaching naked-eye brightness.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb treats the combination of rapid brightening and distinctly blue color as another extreme behavior, potentially hinting at non-standard physics or energy sources.
  • Most explanations focus on a gas-dominated coma. As different volatiles become active at different distances from the Sun, their emission lines can change the apparent color and brightness.
  • From this perspective, the color and brightness evolution reflect an unusually volatile-rich surface and specific chemistry, not necessarily anything artificial.

10. Jet Mass Flux Requiring Unreasonably Large Surface Area

Loeb’s explanation

The jets of gas and dust streaming from 3I/ATLAS, both toward and away from the Sun, seem to imply a mass outflow so strong that, if powered solely by sunlight, it would require a very large active surface area. Loeb argues that this demand on active area is “unreasonable” given typical assumptions about comet nuclei.

How the media usually describe it

  • “Kilometer-scale tails and multiple jets.”
  • “A tail that keeps growing even as it recedes from the Sun.”
  • Reports that the comet appeared to “hide its tail” at times, then reveal a long, structured tail again.

Observations and instruments

  • Long-exposure images reveal jets extending on the order of a million kilometers sunward and several million kilometers anti-sunward, documented and discussed in Loeb’s Medium post on the large-scale structure of the anti-tail.
  • Solar-viewing missions and ground-based telescopes show the tail evolving from a short, subtle elongation into a long ion tail with distinct structure.
  • Earlier in the apparition, images show the comet “growing a tail,” transitioning from a mostly featureless coma to a more classic tail structure.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb uses the strength and extent of the jets to argue that simple sunlight-driven sublimation may not be sufficient, at least under conventional assumptions about the nucleus size and albedo.
  • Comet physicists propose that high-volatility ices such as CO₂ and CO, efficient ionization by solar radiation and solar wind, and a relatively small but highly active area can together produce long, bright ion tails without invoking exotic energy sources.
  • The puzzle here is quantitative rather than qualitative. Jets themselves are expected, but their inferred intensity relative to plausible active area is the point of contention.

11. Non-Gravitational Acceleration Without Corresponding Mass Loss

Loeb’s explanation

Orbit fits that include non-gravitational terms, a standard approach for active comets, indicate that 3I/ATLAS experienced a small extra “push” beyond pure gravity near perihelion. Loeb argues that the magnitude of this acceleration would normally imply that the comet lost at least about 13 percent of its mass, yet images show no obvious fragmentation or large debris cloud.

How the media usually describe it

  • A comet that “is being nudged off its predicted path,” reminiscent of ʻOumuamua’s slight non-gravitational acceleration.

Observations and instruments

  • Orbital solutions incorporate outgassing terms that produce a better fit to positional data than gravity alone, suggesting a modest non-gravitational component to the motion.
  • Pre- and post-perihelion imaging from space and ground shows a single, intact coma rather than a swarm of fragments or a greatly enlarged debris field.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb sees another parallel with ʻOumuamua: a small but measurable non-gravitational acceleration that seems hard to reconcile with the visible level of disruption.
  • The standard cometary view is that non-gravitational accelerations are routine for active comets, and that mass-loss estimates depend heavily on assumptions about gas distribution and jet geometry. A strong, localized jet could impart a noticeable push without catastrophic breakup.

12. Jet Orientation Not Smeared by Rotation

Loeb’s explanation

Photometric data suggest that 3I/ATLAS rotates roughly once every 16 hours. Loeb points out that, despite this, the jets appear as narrow, straight features maintaining their orientation over distances of order a million kilometers rather than being smeared into broad fans, which is what one might expect if they were tied to fixed surface spots on a spinning body. He discusses this in detail in “The 12th Anomaly of 3I/ATLAS” .

How the media usually describe it

  • “Jets that stay sharply lined up even though the comet is spinning.”
  • “A network of jets that looks too straight and stable.”

Observations and instruments

  • Light-curve analyses yield a rotation period near 16 hours based on periodic brightness variations in earlier observations.
  • Long-exposure images taken hours apart show jets at similar position angles, extending over large distances without obvious sweeping patterns.

Explanations and interpretations

  • Loeb suggests that this could be explained if the jets function more like thrusters, actively maintaining an orientation rather than simply venting from rotating surface features.
  • Natural alternatives include jets emerging near the rotation axis, which would change apparent direction very little, complex spin states, or line-of-sight projection effects that minimize visible smearing.
  • As with several other anomalies, the question is whether the observed pattern is genuinely incompatible with standard comet physics or simply lies at an extreme of what is possible.

Miscellaneous and Additional Observed Oddities

Some aspects of 3I/ATLAS do not map neatly to a single item in Loeb’s list, or are better thought of as context for its overall strangeness rather than discrete “anomalies.”

A. Radiation-Processed CO₂ “Crust”

Observations

  • JWST and related modeling suggest that the outer tens of meters of the object may have been heavily processed by cosmic rays over billions of years, building up a CO₂-rich layer. This is described in NASA materials and in a JWST-led paper on the high CO₂/H₂O ratio (JWST ISO analysis, LiveScience JWST article).

Interpretation

  • This picture fits naturally with a very old object that has wandered through interstellar space for a long time. It complements, rather than contradicts, the low-water anomaly.

B. First Radio Detection from an Interstellar Comet

Observations

  • Measurements from the MeerKAT radio array detected hydroxyl (OH) associated with water photodissociation in 3I/ATLAS, the first time radio waves of this kind have been recorded from an interstellar comet (LiveScience radio-detection report).

Interpretation

  • Scientifically, it is a milestone but not a mystery. It confirms that at least some familiar cometary chemistry is operating here.

C. Apparent “Lost Tail” and Shape-Shifting

Observations

  • At various points in its journey, 3I/ATLAS appeared to “lose” its tail in some images, only to reveal a long tail again later. Public-facing coverage sometimes describes this as the comet changing shape.

Interpretation

  • This is largely attributed to viewing geometry and detection thresholds. When the tail is aligned with the line of sight or faint relative to the coma, it can become hard to see even if material is still streaming out. Technically, this is usually filed under changes in tail morphology and visibility, connecting back to the jet and tail anomalies above.

D. Extreme Age and Galactic Origin

Observations

  • Dynamical modeling suggests that 3I/ATLAS likely comes from the Milky Way’s thick disk and may be older than the solar system itself. Some work places its age at roughly seven billion years or more, as reported in Chron.com’s chemistry feature and related technical papers.

Interpretation

  • That would make it the oldest macroscopic object humanity has ever studied up close. This context helps explain many of its unusual chemical and physical features: billions of years of exposure in different Galactic environments can plausibly produce an object that does not look like anything in the relatively younger, more sheltered population of Solar System comets.

E. Narrative and Institutional Tension

Observations

  • NASA and other agencies consistently describe 3I/ATLAS as a comet whose unusual traits are still broadly consistent with natural explanations, as indicated in their 3I/ATLAS facts and FAQs and in statements to the press.
  • Loeb gathers those unusual traits into a unified list of anomalies and keeps open the possibility that some may hint at alien technology or, at minimum, a very unfamiliar class of interstellar object. He discusses the institutional framing explicitly in “There is Nothing More Deceptive than an Obvious Fact: NASA’s Press Conference on 3I/ATLAS” .

Interpretation

  • Beyond the physics, 3I/ATLAS has become a case study in how scientific institutions and individual researchers frame anomalies. For general readers, the story is not just about one icy body racing through the solar system. It is also about how science responds to data that sit at the edges of what existing theories and institutions expect to see.
